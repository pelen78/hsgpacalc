Step 1
Each subject grade × its GPA weight = Quality Points

ATS does not simply average all grades equally. Rediker assigns each subject a GPA weight — separate from its graduation credits. Core subjects carry a weight of 1.0, while PE and Fine Arts carry less. The semester grade is multiplied by that weight to produce Quality Points.

Step 2
Semester Grade = Q1 × 40% + Q2 × 40% + Exam × 20%

Before weights are applied, Rediker calculates the semester grade (S1/S2) from the quarterly grades. If there is no exam, the formula is simply (Q1 + Q2) ÷ 2. If there is a semester exam, Q1 and Q2 each count 40% and the exam counts 20%.

Without exam: (Q1 + Q2) ÷ 2
With exam: Q1 × 0.40 + Q2 × 0.40 + Exam × 0.20
Step 3
Simple GPA = Total Quality Points ÷ Total Weight

All quality points for all courses are added together and divided by the total GPA weight — not the total credits. This is what Rediker calls Simple GPA (on the 0–100 scale).

Full year quality points: 485.5 + 530.2 = 1015.7
Full year weight: 7.9 + 5.9 = 13.8
Simple GPA: 1015.7 ÷ 13.8 = 73.6

Credits (0.25 / 0.50) are only used to determine graduation requirements. GPA weight (0.40 / 0.50 / 1.0) is what determines how much each subject pulls your average.

Step 4
Cumulative GPA = All semesters combined

The same calculation is applied to every semester across all years. All quality points are added and divided by all accumulated weight. This gives the overall Cumulative Simple GPA shown at the top of this tool.

9th year quality points: 1015.7 (weight 13.8)
10th year quality points: … (weight …)
Cumulative GPA: Σ QP ÷ Σ Weight (all years) = GPA

Step 5
Converting to the 4.0 GPA scale

Once the weighted average (0–100) is calculated, it is converted to the 4.0 GPA scale using the standard ATS conversion table. A 73.9 weighted average falls in the C range → 2.0 GPA. This is the scale used for university applications and academic standing.

Why does this scale matter?

The 4.0 GPA scale is the standard at U.S. universities and American international schools. It allows academic performance to be compared across institutions with different grading systems.
